What is the 5/24 Rule?

Chase has an unofficial rule that they will deny your application if you have opened 5 or more personal credit cards (from any bank) in the past 24 months.

Why does this matter?

  • Business cards from most issuers (except Capital One and Discover) usually don't count towards this limit.
  • Authorized user accounts might be counted by Chase's automated system but can often be reconsidered via a phone call.
